# Case Study: UPI Fraud

## Introduction
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India. It involves tricking users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details through fake messages, calls, or links.

## How the Crime Happens
1. The attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media message.
2. They pretend to be a bank official, customer support agent, or a known person.
3. The victim is asked to click on a malicious link or scan a QR code.
4. The attacker convinces the victim to enter their UPI PIN or approve a payment request.
5. Once the victim completes the action, money is instantly transferred from their account to the attacker.

## Targeted Individuals
UPI fraud commonly targets students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment security. People who frequently use online payment apps are also at higher risk.

## Consequences
The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. In some cases, victims may not be able to recover their money. It can also lead to misuse of personal information for further fraud
